Frequently asked questions

Why are the newlines are rendered correctly in cheat sheet, and what can I do?

This is because the cheat sheet does not render its content raw. Before any newline, you have to add a backslash character for it to work (due to the way the module zero-md and MathJax render newlines).
In case you want to do newlines in your $\LaTeX$ formula, you need to put three backslashes instead of two. 

                \begin{cases}
                    y=2x-4\\\
                    7y-9x+9=0
                \end{cases}
This will render in a cheat sheet as $\begin{cases}y=2x-4\\7y-9x+9=0\end{cases}$

What am I supposed to do for my cheat sheet to get into the displayed categories in the Cheat Sheet Market (CSM)?

Easy, you just need to have the correct keywords in your cheat sheet's context!
However, they must all be separated by a space;
keywords on this website are conventionally written in kebab-case: words are in lowercase and separated using hyphens (-). If you do not respect these conventions, you will get less visibility.
Here are the different keywords that are (or are going to be) implemented in the CSM. 

            maths (arithmetic, algebra, geometry...)
            physics
            chemistry
            geography
            foreign-languages (english, spanish, german, french...)
            history (france, england, japan, india...)
            geopolitics
            sociology
            economy
            art / art-history...
            cuisine / gastronomy / recipe...
            technology / it...

Do not forget, that you will have to choose wisely your keywords as you will have a limited amount of characters in your cheat sheet's context! The better keywords you pick, the quicker you will be found.
